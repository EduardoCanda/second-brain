Spoofing a MAC address involves changing the default MAC address assigned to a network interface card (NIC) by its manufacturer. While MAC addresses are hardcoded into the device and cannot be altered permanently, users can temporarily change or 'spoof' them through software. This is often done for privacy reasons, to bypass MAC address filters, or for testing network security. When spoofing, the device presents a different MAC address to the network, which can help in concealing the true identity of the device on the network. However, it's important to note that while spoofing can enhance privacy, it may violate the terms of service of some networks and can lead to unintended consequences.
